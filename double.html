I used to be an English Major.
I loved what I studied, and for further interest, I'd also chosen to study abroad in UBC. I've undergone several steps of considering which kind of branch of studies truely interested me, and what intrigued me most was Applied Linguistics. AL studies from how people acquire language, basically, to how we should apply our language mechanisms in real-life, such as education, computation, or anaylze linguistic data etc. This branch of study needs further study, because analyzing our logical thoughts in learning actually has close relations to neuro-science and machine-learning.
Other branches I loved to study was Literature. Actually, I really hated and avoided literature courses when I was freshman because it seemed so boring unpratical, even useless to my livings. However, after I took prof. Shawn's literature courses, I've got new point of views and inspirations. Literature strongly trains our creative thoughts and critical thinking, and takes us to eras and thoughts that we haven't experienced.
